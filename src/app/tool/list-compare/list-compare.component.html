We can break the comparison of two lists into four parts:

  1. Items Only in ListA

    These are the elements that are present in ListA but not in ListB.

    Steps

    Subtract the elements of ListB from ListA.

    Example

    • ListA: ["apple", "banana", "mango"]
    • ListB: ["banana", "mango", "pineapple", "Grapes"]

    Unique items in ListA: ["apple"]

  2. Items Only in ListB

    These are the elements that are present in ListB but not in ListA.

    Steps

    Subtract the elements of ListA from ListB.

    Example

    Unique items in ListB: ["pineapple", "Grapes"]

  3. Items in Both ListA and ListB

    These are the elements that exist in both ListA and ListB (intersection).

    Steps

    Find the common elements in both lists.

    Example

    Common items: ["banana", "mango"]

  4. Items in Either ListA or ListB

    These are the elements that exist in at least one of the lists (union).

    Steps

    Combine all unique elements from both lists.

    Example

    Union of ListA and ListB: ["apple", "banana", "mango", "pineapple", "Grapes"]
